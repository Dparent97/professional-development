## Why This Matters

**The gap I see:** Maritime and industrial workers are behind on technology. They defer to reliability over innovation, are intimidated by AI, and take forever to approve even obsolete equipment. Meanwhile, AI has lowered barriers to building custom applications that could solve real problems.

**My drive:** Help "blue collar workers of all stripes" realize AI isn't intimidating and can be practical. Lower the barrier between "I have a problem" and "I have a tool that solves it."

**My edge:** I'm not a tech person pretending to understand operations. I *am* operations. I understand failure modes, inspection pressure, compliance burden, and what happens when software breaks and you're 200 miles from shore.

## Target Roles (Priority Order)

### 1. Forward Deployed AI / AI Solutions Engineer
**Why it fits:** Customer-facing builder. Requirements in messy environments, stakeholder wrangling, field realities, shipping under constraints. It's "chief engineer, but for AI deployments."

**Example postings:**
- Salesforce: "AI Forward Deployed Engineer" (travel, regulated industries, conversational AI)
- Lockheed Martin: "Forward Deployed AI/ML Engineer Sr" (customer liaison + productionizing ML)

**Interview angle:** "I've shipped safety-critical systems under inspection pressure, with downtime costing real money. I can do the same with AI, and I won't hallucinate my way through an incident review."

**Gap to close:** More hands-on with deployment pipelines, customer-facing communication polish

---

### 2. ML/MLOps Engineer (Maritime/Defense/Industrial Edge)
**Why it fits:** MLOps is ops. Monitoring, reliability, deployment pipelines, failures, telemetry, constraints, compliance. That's my life already, with fewer oil stains.

**Example posting:**
- Anduril: "Senior ML/MLOps Engineer - Maritime Digital Production" (explicitly maritime-focused, deploying and sustaining AI systems)

**Interview angle:** "I understand instrumentation, failure modes, maintenance cycles, and why uptime beats demos. I can translate requirements into engineering reality."

**Gap to close:** CI/CD pipelines, monitoring/observability tools, containerization deeper than basics

---

### 3. AI Product Manager (Industrial/IoT/Operations)
**Why it fits:** Already do product work - prioritization, tradeoffs, roadmap-by-constraint, user feedback loops, risk management. PM for AI needs someone who can say "no" intelligently.

**Example posting:**
- "Senior AI Product Manager Innovation & IoT Solutions"

**Interview angle:** "I've run real operational roadmaps where the users can't just 'refresh the page' when things break."

**Gap to close:** Formal PM frameworks, stakeholder communication at enterprise level
